Victorian Vehicle accidents - The 2020 Edition

Whilst 2020 may have began like every other year, the year was filled with events truly like no other. Our Own Premier, Daniel Andrews, dubbed the events that occured in 2020 as a "1 in 100 year event". With the introduction of the COVID-19 disease, there were a range of changes to the very way of life that we were typically used to. Melbourne in particular had experienced a range of different lockdowns in order to prevent the widespread transmission of the disease. In total Melbourne spent 153 days in 2020 under different lockdown measures and as such we are examining how the effects of these lockdowns. affected mobility and thus vehicle accidents in 2020.

General Overview of The Effect oF Panedemic Measures on Vehicle Accidents

The number of accidents has had quite a consistent trend in relation to the number of accidents that has occured over time in Victoria. Whilst, this might vary or fluctuate depending on seasonal events, for example increased tourist travel to the Grampians during school holidays or slight reductions in December due to Christmas Break. A structural break is an unexpected change over time & we can clearly identify below that 2020 showed to have a clear structral break in the trend associated with Vehicle Accidents. The introduction of Coronavirus to Victorian shores began through Patient-Zero in Melbourne was a man from Wuhan, who flew to Melbourne from Guandong, China on 19 January. Given the unknown risk of the virus & insufficient health care systems to handle a widespread epidemic, government restrictions were implemented once the real threat of the virus was known.

THE METROPOLITAN VS REGIONAL VICTORIA EFFECT OF PANDEMIC MEASURES ON ACCIDENTS


Another interesting elements in relation to the pandemic measures implement in 2020 was the division between between Metropolitan Victoria & Regional Victoria. Whilst lockdown 1 which dated the period of 31st of March to 12th of May had universal rules for both regions, Lockdown 2 , which started from 9th of July to 27th of October had a different set of rules. A ring of steel was the key feature of Melbourne's harshest restrictions last year, as police-staffed highway checkpoints attempted to prevent people from Metropolitan Victoria entering Regional Victoria without exemptions. With differing rules based on differing milestones, Regional Victoria experienced more freedoms than average Victorians from the Metropolitan Region. As such this has in turn caused a disproportional change in the number of accidents per region & how these correlate to previous trends.

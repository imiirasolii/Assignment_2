Victorian Vehicle accidents - The 2020 Edition

Whilst 2020 may have began like every other year, the year was filled with events truly like no other. Our Own Premier, Daniel Andrews, dubbed the events that occured in 2020 as a "1 in 100 year event". With the introduction of the COVID-19 disease, there were a range of changes to the very way of life that we were typically used to. Melbourne in particular had experienced a range of different lockdowns in order to prevent the widespread transmission of the disease. In total Melbourne spent 153 days in 2020 under different lockdown measures and as such we are examining how the effects of these lockdowns affected mobility and thus vehicle accidents in 2020.

THE REGIONAL VS METROPOLITAN VICTORIA EFFECT OF PANDEMIC MEASURES

Another interesting elements in relation to the pandemic measures implement in 2020 was the division between between Metropolitan Victoria & Regional Victoria Whilst lockdown 1 which dated the period of 31st of March to 12th of May had universal rules for both Metropolitan melbourne, Lockdown 2 , which started from 9th of July to 27th of October had a different set of rules. A ring of steel was the key feature of Melbourne's harshest restrictions last year, as police-staffed highway checkpoints attempted to prevent people from Melbourne entering the regional area of victoria without exemptions. With differing rules based on differing milestones, Regional Victoria experienced more freedoms than average Victorians from the Metropolitan Region. As such this has in turn caused a disproportional change in the number of accidents & how these correlate to previous trends.
